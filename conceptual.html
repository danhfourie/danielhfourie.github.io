Conceptual Design with SolidWorks UROP

Most people know SolidWorks as a program which allows them to create accurate 3D models of physical objects and systems. A much less known use of the software is in conceptual design, that is, using the software as a precise and speedy replacement for a whiteboard or a pen and paper to develop a vague idea into a viable mechanism.

I worked with Professor David Gossard of the MIT CADLAB to explore the capabilites and limitations of SolidWorks in this respect. Some of my examples illustrating the possibilities of conceptual design are included below.

I created part, assembly, and layout examples of the Swissors (an ingenious new type of scissors), which will be used in the spring '09 2.007 class taught by Professor Gossard. I also developed virtual work and force analyses demonstrating the Swissors mechanical advantages over normal scissors.
