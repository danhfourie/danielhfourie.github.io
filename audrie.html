3-axis CNC Desktop Mill


For the final project of MAS.863, I designed and built a 3-axis CNC desktop mill. Audrie is constructed from 1/4" thick aluminum plate cut on the waterjet and joined together with captive T-nuts. The machine met its functional requirements of quick assembly (a few hours), large working volume to footprint ratio (13"x8"x4.5" (X Y Z) / 16"x16"x14" (X Y Z) = 0.13), and stiffness. So far, Audrie has been able to machine steel, ABS, wax, and foam and has the capability to mill PCBs. Additionally, vibrations caused by the motor are small.

Each stage rides four bronze bushingss smoothly along the length of two steel bearing shafts. Jamming due to slight misalignments in the shafts is overcome through the use of flexures. Leadscrews attached to compliantly mounted stepper motors drive the stages via brass nuts. Backlash is nearly negligible, but constant force springs can be used to take out any remaining slop. Cranks can be attached to the secondary shafts of the steppers to drive the machine manually, but it is nominally controlled by a CNC system. EMC2 software converts G-code into step and direction signals, which are sent through a parallel port to custom fabricated stepper driver boards.
